About Mangrove Wonorejo

Mangrove Wonorejo spans 200 hectares of pristine coastal ecosystem, with over 150 species of mangrove trees including Rhizophora and Avicennia. This vital habitat has been carefully restored and now serves as Indonesia's premier destination for sustainable ecotourism.

We've planted over 350,000 mangrove seedlings and partnered with 1,000+ companies and institutions. Our mission is to provide transformative nature experiences while advancing coastal conservation and biodiversity protection.

Why Visit Mangrove Wonorejo

Biodiversity & Conservation

Home to 150+ mangrove species, migratory birds, and diverse marine life. Support active conservation efforts.

Ecological Importance

Prevents coastal erosion, absorbs carbon, protects against natural disasters, and sustains local marine ecosystems.

Education & Participation

Learn about environmental conservation, participate in restoration, and connect with nature in meaningful ways.

Sustainable Tourism

Support local communities and sustainable economic development while protecting this precious ecosystem.
